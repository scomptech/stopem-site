# A Breach of Faith: The Illegal Tactics Behind Virginia’s Redistricting Power Grab (HJR 6007)

**To my fellow Virginians,**

I’m writing to you today with a heavy heart, but also with a clear and urgent warning. As a parent who cares about the future we are leaving for our children, and as a conservative who believes in the sanctity of our laws, I have seen enough. 

We are told that **House Joint Resolution 6007** is just another political disagreement. But if you look deeper—beyond the talking points—you will find a pattern of behavior from our leadership in Richmond that isn't just "partisan." According to our own courts, it is **outright illegal**.

Here is the truth about how this "law" was forced upon us, and why it represents a fundamental breakdown of trust in our Commonwealth.

### 1. A Blatant Abuse of the Special Session
Our government has rules for a reason. They exist to prevent those in power from acting like kings. HJR 6007 was forced through during a **Special Session** that was strictly called to handle the state budget. Under the General Assembly's own rules, you cannot just add new, life-altering constitutional amendments to a budget session without a supermajority or unanimous consent.

But that’s exactly what they did. They ignored their own rulebook to bypass the minority and the public. A Tazewell County judge recently called this a **"blatant abuse of power."** If the people we elect to make the laws won't follow the laws themselves, how can we trust them with our districts?

### 2. Attempting to Cheat the "Intervening Election" Rule
The Virginia Constitution is very clear: to change it, an amendment must be passed by two separate sessions of the General Assembly, with a House of Delegates election in between. This is a "cooling off" period to ensure the people agree with the change.

HJR 6007 was passed on **October 31, 2025**. Here’s the trick: early voting for the 2025 election had already begun weeks earlier, on September 19. They waited until the election was already "in progress" to pass the bill, trying to count that very election as the "intervening" one. It was a calculated attempt to rob the voters of their right to a full election cycle of debate. The court saw right through this, ruling that the election was technically already underway and therefore could not serve as the legal bridge. They tried to short-circuit our Constitution to get their way.

### 3. Shutting the Public Out: The Courthouse Deception
Transparency is the only shield we have against corruption. Virginia law (**Section 30-13**) requires that the text of any proposed constitutional amendment be posted at **every single courthouse** in the Commonwealth for a set duration. This is so that every citizen—from the mountains of Tazewell to the shores of Virginia Beach—can read exactly what their government is planning.

They skipped this. They didn't just rush the vote; they actively failed to provide the required legal notice to the public. They kept us in the dark until it was too late. This wasn't an "oversight"—it was a strategy to prevent families like yours and mine from organizing until the ink was dry.

### 4. Overturning the Will of 65% of Virginians
Lest we forget, in 2020, we the people already settled this. By a massive 65% majority, we voted for a bipartisan commission to end gerrymandering. We wanted politicians out of the room. 

This current effort isn't just about "new maps." It is a move to **unilaterally disarm** the voters. They want to trash the independent commission we built and go back to the old, smoky backrooms where politicians pick their voters to ensure they never lose. If this amendment passes, it creates a "10-1" map where conservative voices are effectively deleted from the map of Virginia.

### Conclusion: A Stand for the Rule of Law
This is no longer about "red vs. blue." This is about right vs. wrong. 

When a government tries to bypass its own rules, cheats the timing of elections, and fails to give legal notice to its citizens, it has lost the moral authority to lead. They are treating our Constitution like a suggestion and our communities like a math problem.

I want my children to grow up in a Virginia where the rules apply to everyone—especially those in power. We must stand together on **April 21st** and say "NO" to this illegal power grab. We must protect our families, our traditions, and our right to a vote that actually matters.
